Problems/Challenges

The problems with the current bus transit can be summed up by the following:

What problems is Transit Smart addressing?

Simplifying the already exisiting bus systems for the public.

The biggest challenge was to simplify the route map system. There are multiple bus routes, and multiple bus lines running on similar routes, which creates challenges in categorizing.

Combining the two bus systems into one interface.

There are namely two bus systems running the span of Detroit, DDOT and SMART.
Both of these systems are managed by separate departments and thus operate differently, having a different set of rules, categories, policies etc. There is a clear lack of an efficient bridge between the two.

Improving ease of use and access to already existing users.

More than 50% of the population in Detroit falls under the low income group — which form the primary users of the buses. And about 49% of the households in Detroit do not have access to Wi-Fi. Thus, creating an app or a website was not the answer to provide information. Plus, there are already apps and websites by both DDOT and SMART.

Increasing bus transit footfall by tapping into a potential demographic.

The untapped demographic becomes the youth without cars, out-of-state students, international students, etc. During various discussions and from personal experience, the reason why buses become out of question is the complexity of its use, unavailability of such information.

Addressing inclusive design challenges.

Designing for everyone. Special focus on one of the most commonly overlooked issues in transit systems: color-blindness.
